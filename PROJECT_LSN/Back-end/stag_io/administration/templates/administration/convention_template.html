IT HAS BEEN AGREED AS FOLLOWS  /  IL A ÉTÉ CONVENU CE QUI SUIT :

Article 1 — General Provisions

This agreement is governed by Article 06 of Executive Decree No. 13-306 of 31 August 2013 on the organisation of practical internships in a professional setting for students, and by the Order of 21 January 2015 on the nature and modalities of their evaluation and scheduling.

Article 2 — Purpose of the Agreement

This convention defines the organisational framework for the practical internship carried out by {{ internship.student.full_name }} at {{ internship.company.name }}, formalising the rights, duties, and responsibilities of all parties.

Article 3 — Objectives of the Internship

The internship enables the student to apply theoretical and methodological knowledge in a real professional environment, develop professional competencies, and prepare for working life. The internship subject is: {{ internship.subject }}.

Article 4 — Internship Theme and Work Organisation

The theme, work plan, and assigned objectives are defined in consultation between the university and the host organisation, in line with the student's study programme and the host institution's operational needs. The student shall produce a final internship report upon completion.

Article 5 — Supervision

Each internship benefits from dual supervision:

  • An Academic Supervisor designated by {{ internship.student.university.name }}, responsible for pedagogical follow-up and evaluation.
  • A Host Supervisor designated by {{ internship.company.name }}, responsible for daily on-site supervision.

The student is placed under the hierarchical authority of the host supervisor and must comply with the host institution's internal regulations.

Article 6 — Practical Modalities

  • Duration: {{ duration }} — from {{ internship.start_date|date:"d M Y" }} to {{ internship.end_date|date:"d M Y" }}.
  • The internship is carried out during academic year {{ academic_year }}.

Article 7 — Student Status and Social Protection

The student retains their registered student status throughout the internship. Social security coverage is provided by {{ internship.student.university.name }}. In the event of a work-related accident, the host institution must file the workplace accident declaration with the relevant social security body and send a copy to {{ internship.student.university.name }} without delay.

Article 8 — Conduct, Discipline and Absence

The student is subject to the host institution's internal regulations, including discipline, working hours, safety rules, and professional confidentiality. Any absence, even for medical reasons, must be justified within 72 hours. In the event of serious misconduct, the host institution may terminate the internship after informing the academic supervisor.

Article 9 — Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

Work results are the joint property of {{ internship.student.university.name }} and {{ internship.company.name }}. The internship report must be submitted to the academic supervisor after review by the host supervisor. The report may remain confidential but will be accessible to teaching staff for evaluation. In case of publication, both institutions and supervisors must be credited.

Article 10 — Remuneration and Benefits

Unless agreed otherwise in writing, the student is not entitled to any remuneration from the host institution. The host may, however, provide benefits in kind or a supplementary allowance in accordance with its policies and applicable regulations.

Article 11 — Commitment and Continuity

By signing, the student irrevocably commits to completing the full internship. Unilateral abandonment will result in disqualification from presenting a final-year project during the same academic year and a formal note in the academic record.

Article 12 — Duration and Entry into Force

This convention is valid solely from {{ internship.start_date|date:"d M Y" }} to {{ internship.end_date|date:"d M Y" }} and enters into force upon signature by all parties.
